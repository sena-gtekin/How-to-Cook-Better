Vegan Recipes

Lentil Bolognese

Ingredients

  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (or use more oregano)
  • 1 ½ teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper to taste

Method

  1. First, soak your red lentils in water for at least 30 minutes, then drain. Meanwhile, dice the onions and mince the garlic.
  2. Cook the onions in olive oil in a deep saute pan until a fond starts to appear. Then start deglazing the pan with a few splashes of water.
  3. Cook for a total of 9-10 minutes, continuing to deglaze as needed, until the onions are deeply golden brown.
  4. Add the garlic, dried oregano and thyme, and salt/pepper. Cook for 60-90 seconds, stirring frequently.
  5. Stir the tomato paste into the onions for a few minutes, until darker in color (brick red).
  6. Add in the vegetable broth, soaked red lentils, and finely chopped walnuts.
  7. Cook the lentil mixture at a rapid simmer for 20 minutes.
  8. Stir in the balsamic vinegar and season to taste with salt and pepper.

Vegan Portobello Tacos

Ingredients

  • 2 extra large portobello mushrooms
  • 1/2 an onion – optional
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander
  • salt to taste

Method

  1. Slice the portobellos into ½ inch thick wedges and slice bell pepper in to ½ thick strips. If adding onion, cut into ½ inch thick rings or half moons.
  2. Place all on a sheet-pan lined sheet pan & Mix marinade ingredients together in a small bowl.
  3. Brush both sides of mushrooms liberally with the marinade, then remaining red bell pepper and onion lightly. Sprinkle portobellos with salt. Roast 20 minutes or until portobellos are fork-tender.
  4. While this is roasting, heat the beans any prep any additional garnishes.
  5. Pickled onions and Vegan cilantro Crema both take about 10 minutes to make. Or simply use avocado slices.
  6. When ready to serve, warm the tortillas ( over a gas flame on the stove, or in a toaster oven) and spread generously with the refried black beans
  7. Divide chipotle portobellos and peppers (and onions if used)among the tortillas. Top with Cilantro Crema, Poblano Salsa, or avocado, fresh cilantro and optional pickled onions

Harissa Chicken Traybake

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken legs
  • 4 tbsp harissa paste (rose harissa is nice)
  • 1 garlic bulb, broken into cloves
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • 400g cherry tomatoes on the vine
  • 350g new potatoes, halved if large

Method

  1. Heat oven to 190C/170C fan/gas 5.
  2. Score deep lines all over the chicken legs, then rub in the harissa.
  3. Season well and place in a roasting tin.
  4. Scatter the garlic over and around the chicken.
  5. Squeeze the lemon wedges over, then place them in the tin with the cherry tomatoes, potatoes and olives.
  6. Season, drizzle over the oil and toss briefly to mix everything together.
  7. Cook in the oven for 45 mins-1 hr until the chicken is cooked through and golden.

Spring Roast Chicken

Ingredients

  • 8 chicken thighs, on the bone, skin on
  • 1 lemon, zested and cut into wedges
  • 4 shallots, thickly sliced
  • 4 rosemary sprigs
  • small pack tarragon
  • 450g asparagus spears, trimmed

Method

  1. Heat oven to 200C/180C fan/gas 6.
  2. Put the chicken thighs in a large shallow roasting tin, season and toss with the olive oil, lemon zest and wedges, shallots, rosemary and tarragon.
  3. Arrange the chicken thighs, skin-side up, and roast for 40-50 mins until the skin is crisp and golden.
  4. Meanwhile, bring a large pan of water to the boil and cook the asparagus for 3 mins or until tender, adding the courgettes and peas for the final minute.
  5. Drain the vegetables and toss in with the chicken, coating well with the cooking juices.
  6. Crumble over the feta and serve.

Overnight Oats

Ingredients

  • Rolled oats
  • Milk
  • Yogurt
  • Chia Seeds
  • Maple Syrup

Method

  1. Make sure to use old fashioned rolled oats for the best texture.
  2. And remember to look for certified gluten-free oats if you’re gluten-sensitive or celiac.
  3. You can use dairy or dairy-free milk (like almond milk, oat milk, or cashew milk), and yes, even water works. It just depends how creamy you’d like it.
  4. A simple Greek yogurt or dairy-free yogurt provides extra creaminess, protein, and that perfect touch of tangy flavor.
  5. These magical little seeds will gel up in the oats to make it creamy and thick.
  6. Natural sweeteners like maple syrup or honey blend in beautifully. You can always add as little or as much as you’d like.
